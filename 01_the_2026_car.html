The 2026
F1 car.

Everything changed this year. New engine. New wings. New rules. Here's what it actually means — in plain English.

Why did F1 change everything at once?

The last major rule overhaul was 2022. Before that, 2014. F1 doesn't change things often — but when it does, it goes all in. 2026 is the biggest single-season reset in F1 history. New engine rules AND new aero rules, simultaneously.

2014
The hybrid era begins
F1 switched from V8 engines to 1.6L V6 turbos with electric motors. Everyone complained they were too quiet. Mercedes dominated for a decade.
2022
Ground effect returns
The cars got huge tunnels under the floor to create downforce. Cars could follow each other more closely. Porpoising became a thing. Red Bull dominated.
2026 — NOW
Everything resets
New engine, new aero, new ways to overtake, new manufacturers joining. Every team starts from zero. The pecking order could flip completely.
🎯
Three reasons F1 changed everything: (1) attract new manufacturers like Audi and Ford, (2) make overtaking easier, (3) use sustainable fuel as part of a net-zero target by 2030. It's racing meets politics meets engineering.
Half electric. Half petrol. All mental.

The 2026 power unit is a genuine step change. For the first time ever, roughly half the power comes from electricity. The old cars were maybe 20% electric. This is a completely different beast.

2014–2025 ENGINE
~550kW from petrol combustion
~120kW from electric motor
~80% petrol / 20% electric split
MGU-H (exhaust energy recovery) — very complex
4.5MJ energy recovered per lap
2026 ENGINE — NEW
~400kW from petrol combustion
~350kW from electric motor (3× more!)
~50/50 petrol / electric split
MGU-H removed — simpler, cheaper
8.5MJ recovered per lap (doubled)
⚡
Total power is still over 1,000 horsepower — but half of it is now electric. Think of it like a Formula 1 hybrid car, not a petrol car with a small battery bolted on. This is why Audi, Honda, and Ford all wanted in — it's far more relevant to road car technology they're actually developing.
1,000+
Horsepower total — same ballpark, completely different source
50/50
Electric vs petrol power split — a first in F1 history
100%
Sustainable fuel — fully carbon-neutral, no fossil fuels
3×
More electrical power than before — electric motor nearly tripled
⛽ THE FUEL THING
The 2026 cars run on 100% sustainable fuel — no fossil fuels at all. The fuel is made from things like captured CO₂, municipal waste, or non-food crops. It still burns in the engine the same way — it just doesn't dig new carbon out of the ground. Lewis Hamilton said in testing: "I sat in a meeting about it and felt like I needed a degree."
DRS is dead. Long live Active Aero.

For 12 years, DRS was F1's overtaking aid — a flap on the rear wing that opened on straights to reduce drag. It's gone. Replaced by something smarter: wings that automatically adjust themselves throughout every single lap.

ACTIVE AERO — INTERACTIVE
🔄 Corner Mode
⚡ Straight Mode
🏎
↗↗ GRIP
Wings CLOSED — maximum downforce for cornering grip
TOP SPEED280 km/h
In corners, wings stay closed and angled. This creates maximum downforce — pushing the car into the road so it can take corners at insane speeds without sliding.
🔑
Key difference from DRS: DRS only opened the rear wing, only on specific straights, only if you were within 1 second of the car ahead. Active Aero opens both front and rear wings, on every straight, for every driver on every lap. No need to be chasing someone. It's built into the car's normal operation.
📍 STRAIGHT MODE BOARDS
At each track you'll now see signs marked "SM" — Straight Mode. That's where the wings open. Previously they said "DRS". Same idea, different (and actually better) system. The car's computer handles the transition automatically.
The new ways to attack and defend

With so much more electrical energy available, drivers now have two new power tools — one for everyone, one for the car doing the chasing.

⚡
Boost Mode
Available to every driver, any time. Lets you take manual control of your electric energy deployment — use it to attack, defend, or just go faster wherever you want on track. Think of it as squeezing extra electricity from the battery for a burst of pace.
ALL DRIVERS · ANY LAP
🚀
Overtake Mode
The DRS replacement for close racing. If you're within 1 second of the car ahead when you pass the detection point (usually the final corner), you get extra electrical power for the whole of the next lap. You can harvest 0.5MJ more energy AND deploy it at a higher rate — sustained higher speed down the straight.
WITHIN 1 SECOND ONLY
🔋
Recharge Mode
When you lift off the throttle at the end of a straight, the car harvests electrical energy back into the battery. It's like regenerative braking on an electric car — but at 300km/h. The catch: using Recharge mode disables the Active Aero devices temporarily.
HARVESTING · LIFT-OFF
🎮
Managing these three modes is now a huge part of driving an F1 car. When do you use Boost? When do you save battery? Do you recharge now and lose aero, or stay open? Drivers are essentially managing a complex energy budget at 300km/h while also, you know, racing 19 other cars.
The cars got smaller. That's a big deal.

F1 cars have been getting bigger and heavier for 20 years. The 2026 regs reversed that. Shorter, narrower, lighter — and that changes how the racing feels.

−200mm
Shorter wheelbase — 3400mm vs 3600mm. More nimble through tight corners.
−30kg
Lighter — minimum weight down from 798kg to 768kg. Easier to accelerate.
−15%
Less downforce overall — but cleaner air behind the car for overtaking.
90%
Downforce retained when 20m behind a rival (up from 70% by end of 2025).
💨
Why the 90% number matters: by the end of 2025, if you drove 20 metres behind another car, you lost 30% of your downforce from the turbulent air ahead. Corners became almost undriveable. The 2026 cars are designed so that dirty air affects you far less — making it easier to follow closely and actually attempt an overtake.
🏁
Simpler wings
Fewer blade elements on both front and rear. Less turbulent "outwash" air being thrown behind the car. The car following you suffers less.
BETTER FOR FOLLOWING
🚗
Flat floor returns
The big Venturi tunnels under the 2022–2025 cars are gone. Flatter floor with a larger diffuser at the rear. Bye bye porpoising.
NO MORE BOUNCING
🧱
Safer structure
Two-stage nose cone design. Better side impact protection around the driver and fuel cell. New external lights show ERS status to marshals.
SAFER CRASHES
🏭
New manufacturers
Audi, Ford (with Red Bull), and Honda all joined because of these rules. More manufacturer variety = more competitive engine battles.
MORE COMPETITION
Quick quiz 🏁

Three questions on the 2026 rules. Don't worry if you get one wrong — even Hamilton said he needed a degree to understand all this.

Q1. DRS is gone in 2026. What replaced it?
A Nothing — F1 just accepts overtaking will be harder
B Active Aero — wings that automatically open on every straight for every driver
C A push-to-pass button that works once per lap
✅ Correct! Active Aero is better than DRS because it works for everyone on every straight — not just the car chasing within 1 second. Straight Mode opens both wings automatically, reducing drag and boosting top speed without any driver input needed.
❌ Active Aero replaced DRS. Both front and rear wings now open automatically on designated straights — for every driver, every lap. No need to be within 1 second of anyone. It's built into how the car operates.
Q2. What's the electric/petrol power split in the 2026 engine?
A 80% petrol, 20% electric — same as before
B 50% petrol, 50% electric — a genuine half-and-half split
C 100% electric — F1 went full EV
✅ Right! Roughly 50/50. The ICE output dropped while the electric motor nearly tripled in power. This is why manufacturers like Audi and Ford were attracted — it's far more relevant to the electric cars they're developing for road use.
❌ It's roughly 50/50. The petrol engine actually produces less power than before, while the electric motor's output nearly tripled. Total power is still over 1,000hp — just from a very different mix of sources.
Q3. What is Overtake Mode and when can a driver use it?
A A speed boost available any time the driver wants it
B Extra electrical power available for the next lap if you're within 1 second of the car ahead at the detection point
C A penalty mode that slows the car in front to even up the racing
✅ Exactly! It replaced the "within 1 second" aspect of DRS. Pass the detection point (usually the last corner) within 1 second of the car ahead, and you get extra electric power for the whole next lap — more energy to harvest AND more power to deploy.
❌ Overtake Mode is conditional. You have to be within 1 second of the car ahead at a specific detection point — usually the last corner before a long straight. Get there in time, and you get extra electrical power for the entire next lap.
You now understand F1's biggest ever reset. 🏆

Next time someone asks "why does that car suddenly go faster on the straight?" — you'll actually know.
